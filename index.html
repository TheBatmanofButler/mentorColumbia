mentors@Columbia

As a first-year at Columbia, I had no idea what I wanted to do. All I knew was that I liked coding and I was pretty good at it. Over the last two years, I've learned a lot about the different paths available in the tech industry and how to access them, and a lot of that credit goes to upperclass friends who shared their expertise and mistakes with me.

Mentors@Columbia is a program designed to help students learn about the different opportunities available in tech, on a one-on-one basis. Mentors come from a wide range of backgrounds and interests, and they hold office hours throughout the week at different places on Columbia's campus. Come through to learn more about tech internships, what classes to take, or just chat.
